Chapter XXVI. – Confrontation

While she froze in shock, he merely straightened lazily, as though she had interrupted him during a calm moment of observation. A goblet of wine rested in his hand as he stood beside the bay window where shadows blended into the cold stone. His shirt hung loosely open, and in his eyes lingered that familiar, unpleasantly amused expression. “Aaliyah,” he drawled her name with that characteristic smirk of his, mixing irony, laziness, and deliberate superiority. “The world really is small, huh?” Aaliyah remained rooted in place. Her heart pounded wildly. Her throat tightened as though she had forgotten how to swallow. Two years. Two years without a word. And now he stood there as though nothing had happened — looking as though he found the situation entertaining. “I didn’t know you were here…” Her voice came out shakier than she wanted. Everything she had convinced herself of over those months shattered within seconds. “Really?” He raised an eyebrow and took a sip from the goblet. “And yet I’ve been here quite a while.” “Why did you leave without saying anything?” The question was quiet, but direct. Pain lingered openly beneath her voice, despite all the time she had spent trying to bury it. William laughed softly. Cruelly. “Aaliyah, seriously?” he said. “You’re really going to play the abandoned lover right now?” He leaned slightly closer, as though trying to swallow her into the shadows around him. “I thought we understood each other back then. You were a distraction. A pretty, willing, pleasant distraction. Long evenings in the desert get exhausting. You made them more bearable. Nothing more.” His gaze moved across her face without the slightest trace of tenderness. “And now you want an explanation from me?” He tilted his head. “For what? For stopping the use of something that stopped entertaining me?” Aaliyah felt her chin tremble, but she held herself together. She would not let him break her again. Not in front of him. “You didn’t have to disappear.” The words came quietly. But firmly. “I didn’t disappear,” he corrected coldly. “I simply left for something better. And honestly? I don’t understand why I should’ve explained it to you back then.” His gaze was hard. Merciless. “But admit it — what bothers you more? That I left you… or that I never looked back?” Aaliyah flushed red. Humiliation. Anger. William merely chuckled under his breath and turned his attention back toward the window. “You should go. Castle corridors aren’t the right place for nostalgic outbursts.” His lips curved faintly. “Besides… if you’re here with the people I think you are, perhaps someone is already missing you.” The tone of his voice carried unmistakable implication. Aaliyah said nothing. She turned sharply and walked away quickly because inside her, confusion was rising again — confusion she never wanted to feel again. Behind her, she heard his quiet laughter. And she knew that was exactly what he wanted. Aaliyah moved through the corridors as though trapped in a dream. Her legs carried her without direction, barely aware of where she was going. Every step hurt — not physically, but somewhere deeper, in places she had believed healed long ago. Two years. Two years of silence. And all it took was a few words, one look from those arrogant eyes… and she felt like that girl again, silently begging to be loved. William. His name remained lodged inside her heart like broken glass. Only moments ago he had looked at her with that same mocking smile, the same one he used to wear while leaving her waiting for hours. He spoke smoothly, elegantly — and yet every word was poisonous. Every syllable sounded like mockery. Like a reminder that he could do whatever he wanted with her… and perhaps still could. She stopped and leaned heavily against a stone wall. Her fingers clenched the fabric of her cloak so tightly her knuckles turned white. I’m stronger now, she told herself silently. I’m not the same person anymore. But even strong women have days when their voices tremble. Days when the past catches up to them. She was shaking. With anger. With shame. With the shock that he could still hurt her at all. But worse than his words was her own reaction. She wanted to scream at him. Wanted to hit him. Wanted to tell him she no longer cared about him at all. But instead, she had stood there in silence. And once again, he had the upper hand. Tears began slipping down her face quietly, without a single sob. She did not want to cry. Not because of him. But something inside her had broken. Irreversibly. James walked through the silent castle corridors while torchlight painted golden shadows across the stone walls. It was not fear driving him forward. It was restlessness. He stopped, leaning one shoulder against a pillar, and closed his eyes for a moment. That was when he heard it. Soft, muffled sobbing. He turned — and saw her. Aaliyah. She stood at the far end of the corridor in a long cloak without the hood raised. Her face was pale, her eyes red, her lips tightly pressed together as though refusing to let out a single sound. Her body trembled beneath restrained emotion. “Aaliyah?” he called softly. She flinched and quickly tried to wipe away her tears with the back of her hand, forcing herself upright. “Sorry… I… I just needed some air.” James said nothing. He simply walked slowly toward her and stopped directly in front of her. He looked into her eyes — and saw everything. The pain she had hidden for so long. “You saw him,” he said quietly. She did not answer. She only nodded and closed her eyes. “Was he the same as you remembered?” Aaliyah let out a bitter laugh. “Worse. Two minutes, and he already had me in his grasp again.” James tightened his jaw. Then he lifted a hand — but instead of touching her, he slowly lowered it again. “He doesn’t have you in his grasp anymore.” “I’m saying it because I trust you,” she continued quietly. “Because every time things fell apart, you stayed.” James held her gaze without speaking. Torchlight flickered across the stone walls around them, painting shifting gold across his face, but he barely seemed to notice it. For a moment, the entire corridor felt strangely distant. “You don’t have to decide anything tonight,” he said at last, his voice low and careful. “Not after… all this.” A faint, tired smile touched Aaliyah’s lips. “That’s the thing,” she admitted softly. “I think I decided a long time ago. I was just too afraid to admit it.” Something in James’s expression changed then. The guarded restraint he carried so often seemed to loosen, if only slightly. Slowly, cautiously, he reached for her hand. This time, he did touch her. His fingers closed gently around hers, warm and steady, as though giving her every chance to pull away if she wished. Aaliyah didn’t. Instead, her hand tightened around his. For a brief moment, neither of them moved. The silence between them no longer felt heavy. It felt safe. James let out a quiet breath, almost disbelieving. “You have no idea how long I’ve wanted to hear that.” Aaliyah looked at him, and for the first time that evening, the pain inside her chest loosened enough to let something warmer through. “I think maybe I do,” she whispered. A soft laugh escaped him then — small, genuine, rare. He lifted her hand slightly, brushing his thumb gently across her knuckles. “No more running after people who make you beg for scraps of affection,” he said quietly. “You deserve better than that.” Emotion flickered across Aaliyah’s face again, but this time it wasn’t shame or heartbreak. It was relief. She stepped closer to him slowly, almost uncertainly at first, until the space between them disappeared. James’s free hand instinctively settled against her waist, careful and restrained, as though he still could not quite believe she was truly there with him like this. Aaliyah rested her forehead lightly against his chest and closed her eyes. And for the first time in a very long while — she didn’t feel alone. Aaliyah’s breath caught softly. For a heartbeat, she only looked at him — at the careful restraint in his eyes, at the patience in the way he waited for her answer instead of taking what he wanted. And that alone made something inside her ache in the gentlest possible way. A faint smile touched her lips. “You may,” she whispered. James exhaled quietly, almost as if he had been holding that breath for far too long. Then he moved closer. Slowly. Giving her every chance to change her mind. Aaliyah didn’t step away. When his hand slid from her cheek to the side of her neck, the touch was warm and careful, nothing like the possessive grasp she remembered from another life. His forehead brushed hers for the briefest moment before his lips finally met hers. The kiss was soft. Tentative at first, as though neither of them wanted to break the fragile honesty that had settled between them. Aaliyah felt her fingers tighten around his hand instinctively. Warmth spread through her chest, melting the last remnants of cold anger and humiliation William had left behind. James kissed her again, slightly deeper this time, no urgency in it — only sincerity. Months of silent devotion hidden beneath restraint. When they finally parted, they remained close enough that their breaths still mingled in the cool night air. James rested his forehead lightly against hers again and let out a quiet, almost disbelieving laugh. “I’ve imagined doing that for a very long time,” he admitted softly. Aaliyah smiled properly for the first time that evening. “I know,” she whispered. It was the only moment when a flicker of her old uncertainty appeared in her eyes. But only for a second. Then she gave a quiet nod. And so he kissed her. Gently. It was not a kiss consumed by passion, but one filled with trust, tenderness, and quiet admiration. When they finally pulled apart, neither of them spoke for a moment. They simply breathed. The air between them felt different now. As though the world itself had changed — or perhaps only the two of them had. A faint smile touched James’s lips. Then he drew a soft breath and looked at her. “Come on,” he said quietly. “I’ll walk you back.” They moved through the corridors slowly, peacefully, shoulder to shoulder. When they reached her door, James stopped. He did not step closer. Nor away. He gave her space — just as he always did. “Good night, Aaliyah,” he said calmly. “Good night, James,” she answered with a gentle smile. For a brief moment, their eyes met again. Then Aaliyah stepped into her room and quietly closed the door behind her. James remained standing in the corridor for several seconds longer. Only then did he finally turn and walk away.
