Chapter XVIII. – The road into shadows

Morning arrived quietly. A cold wind rose above the desert, carrying fine grains of sand that whispered against the fabric of the tents. The oasis still slept, yet within its heart, an uneasy spark already smoldered, waiting to ignite. James stood at the entrance of the tent, watching the horizon where the first rays of sunlight slowly began to appear. Beside him, Rowena prepared for the journey ahead. Aaliyah woke last. Her body was still exhausted, but her mind raced relentlessly. She knew that everything that had happened was no coincidence. The oasis, the Spring of Knowledge, even Zarveth slowly creeping through the shadows — all of it had woven itself into a single, unavoidable thread of fate. Harim waited for them at the southern gate. Leaning on his staff, he wore a serious expression, though his eyes held the calmness of a man accustomed to farewells. “Take care of yourselves. Darkness walks behind you,” he said quietly. Rowena stepped toward him and bowed her head slightly. “Thank you for the shelter… and for the warning.” Aaliyah smiled faintly, this time without her usual teasing edge. “Keep the spring safe, Harim. I’d rather not come back to ashes.” Harim returned the smile softly. “The spring will be waiting. I’ll protect it with my life.” “We need to move,” James said in a low voice. “Darkness does not wait, and the road to Stonegard Castle is long.” With those words, they stepped into the morning light, every footstep carrying the weight of fate — but also the determination not to break. Behind them remained the oasis — the place where their struggle had begun, and where shadows still lingered, waiting for the chance to consume them.
