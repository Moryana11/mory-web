Chapter XV. – Whispering currents and the shadow in the sand

Rowena awoke suddenly, as though something had called to her. Silence filled the tent. The orb inside her bag glowed faintly, as if it too could sense the call. Slowly, she reached for it and took it into her palm. Its light grew brighter, reacting to her touch. Carefully, she rose to her feet, draped her cloak over herself, and slipped past the sleeping Aaliyah. The tent rustled softly behind her. Again, the voice — closer now, clearer. It guided her farther away, beyond the fig trees. Toward the old sanctuary with the spring… Rowena approached slowly, her heart beating in a strange rhythm as the orb grew warm, glowing with golden-white light. She stepped inside. In the coolness of the stone, she felt something like a breath — a faint current brushing against her neck. At the center of the sanctuary lay the spring. Crystal-clear water swirled peacefully within a small stone basin. Rowena knelt beside it, placed a hand on the edge, and lowered her head. And then the water spoke. “I remember you, Child of Light,” said a voice that came not from outside, but directly into her mind. She remained calm. “Why did you call me?” she whispered. “I do not call. I answer. You were the one asking — even without words. And I know what you seek.” “Morr’kalil,” she spoke the name again, the same name that had lodged itself like a thorn within her dreams. “His name is older than the sands of this land. His hunger endures.” The water rippled and revealed images: ancient gates, corrupted temple walls, figures in black cloaks, eyes without lenses staring from the depths, and a silent calling echoing across the ages. “Where are we supposed to go?” Rowena asked, and her orb shone brighter still. “To the place where stone drank blood and swords raised walls. To the place where human pride stood against the gods. To the place where the first seal trembled.” The voice faded, but the ripples in the water formed a pattern — a map. Not complete, but enough to guide them. Rowena leaned closer to the image, her eyes tracing the shifting lines across the water’s surface. “That’s the castle… Stonegard Castle,” she said quietly, her voice filled with a certainty born from instinct. Rowena rose silently, hid the orb beneath her cloak, and returned to the tent. Certainty reflected in her eyes now — she knew where they had to go next.
