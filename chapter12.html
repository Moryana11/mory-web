Chapter XII. – Shadow above the sand

The sun slowly drifted toward the horizon, bathing the desert in a crimson-orange glow. Every step through the sand felt heavier, every breath of dry air sharper. And yet, when the outlines of an oasis finally appeared in the distance, their hearts filled with relief. The Sanctuary Oasis revealed itself like a miracle amidst the wasteland — enclosed between steep cliffs whose ridges rose into the sky like stone guardians. From a bird’s-eye view, it would have resembled an emerald jewel set within the pale golden crown of the desert. The smooth cliff walls protected the oasis from fierce winds and the eyes of unwanted visitors. It was precisely because of this hidden location that it had become a safe haven for travelers from distant lands. Between palm groves, a lake of crystal-clear water, and countless tents stretched a marketplace — bustling and alive like a beating heart. Caravans with wagons burdened by goods regularly arrived from the surrounding roads. Heavy sacks of spices, fabrics, and precious metals were unloaded from camels while merchants arranged wares from both east and west across their stalls. The scent of dried dates, roasted nuts, and spiced tea mingled with the smell of sweat and leather. Every corner of the oasis was alive — people haggled loudly, children ran laughing between the stalls, and old storytellers recounted tales whose echoes carried all the way to the stone sanctuary at the oasis’s far end. The Sanctuary Oasis was more than a resting place. It was a crossroads between worlds, a hidden nexus of ancient trade routes, and living proof that even in the middle of nowhere, something sacred could still emerge. “So this is it… the Sanctuary Oasis,” Aaliyah whispered, her voice filled with both awe and exhaustion. James nodded silently, his eyes never stopping their scan of the horizon. As beautiful as the place was, his instincts warned him. Something was wrong here. Rowena stopped atop one of the dunes. Turning toward the wind, she closed her eyes. “The air… it’s different. It smells of magic, but not only the pure kind.” James stepped beside her. “I feel it too. Like residual energy — twisted and rotten.” “It’s the Shadow,” Aaliyah said quietly. “It’s been creeping behind us ever since we left the temple.” Without warning, the wind rose violently, and a pillar began to form from the sand — a swirling vortex of dark dust and shadows. At its center, the vague outline of a face slowly emerged — only a shadow, only a fleeting glimpse — and then two golden eyes ignited within it, piercing and cold.
