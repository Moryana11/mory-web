Chapter IV. – The Girl Who Burned

Flashback from James’s point of view James stood leaning against the support pole of the tent, cleaning the blade of his dagger. He moved the cloth across the steel slowly and carefully while discreetly watching the woman sitting beside the fire, laughing at one of William’s remarks. Aaliyah. She was like fire itself. Honest, wild, and kind. There was always hope in her voice. And beside her? William. His fellow warrior. Confident, charismatic, admired. Whenever he entered a room, attention naturally shifted toward him like iron drawn to a magnet — and Aaliyah had been no exception. In battle, reliable. In camp, loud. In love… Selfish. James saw it clearly. William did not love her — he loved being loved by her. And slowly, Aaliyah began to disappear. Not physically. Spiritually. There was more pain than light in her magic now. Her laughter had become a strained imitation of joy. And James, always lingering only a few steps behind her, silently watched as her fire slowly began to smolder. One morning, he saw her standing before an empty tent. William was gone. No goodbye. No apology. Only silence and cold emptiness. Aaliyah did not move. She did not need words. She needed someone willing to remain beside her in silence. So that night, he came to her by the fire. “You deserve more than being someone’s trophy,” he told her quietly. She lowered her head. The silence between them felt fragile. “What do you want, James?” she asked sharply. “Reassurance that you’re better than him?” He shook his head. “I want… to protect you. Not like some savior. Just as someone who sees you for who you are. Not because of what you do for everyone else.” It was the bravest thing he had ever said. And also the truest. Her eyes filled with something he recognized immediately — fear, pain, and a quiet, unspoken no. “Don’t say that, James… Please,” she whispered. He respected her decision. He did not try to convince her otherwise. Instead, he looked into her eyes one last time. “I want you to know I’m not expecting anything. But… I’ve spent a long time watching you burn yourself away for other people. And if anyone should be the one protecting you — I’d want it to be me. I’m not pressuring you,” he said quietly. “You don’t have to answer me. You don’t have to decide today or tomorrow. Just think about it.” At the gates of Vienta, he looked into her eyes one final time before walking away. With her face etched into his memory. With the knowledge that he had not been chosen. And yet, with a quiet faith that whatever existed between them — or whatever could have existed — was not truly lost. Then, two years later, he saw flames burning in the desert. And immediately, he knew it was her. No other fire could ever be mistaken for Aaliyah’s. Not even after all those years. She stood with her magical staff in hand, ready to attack, her gaze fixed directly on him.
