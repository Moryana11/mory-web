Chapter XXVII. – The hunt

When Aaliyah closed the door to her room, she leaned against it wearily and closed her eyes for a moment. Inside her, there was silence — not emptiness, but the calm that comes after a storm. James’s words echoed through her thoughts. And yet… she knew the peace within Stonegard Castle was only temporary. While Aaliyah rested quietly, James returned to the courtyard. His gaze lifted toward the darkness above, where the stars glimmered with faint, muted light. Conflicting emotions churned inside him. Happiness — because Aaliyah returned his feelings. That quiet but steadfast bond between them gave him strength and hope after everything they had endured together. But alongside it grew anger. Hatred toward William — the man who had complicated their lives so many times and left deep scars behind. A bitter thought crossed James’s mind before he could stop it: I wish he had never been born. With his fists clenched tightly, he silently vowed that he would do everything within his power to protect Aaliyah and everyone he cared about. No matter what dangers still awaited them. Zarveth walked through the desert. His body was weakened, but his spirit remained unbroken. The darkness sustaining him vibrated with a single overwhelming purpose: to free his master. But he could not accomplish it alone. He needed another Shadow. Another body to serve as a vessel. Then he heard voices. Muted. Close. He stopped atop a dune and looked down upon a campsite below. Seven people — three elderly men and four women — huddled around a small fire. Healers. Traveling toward Stonegard Castle. Peaceful. Vulnerable. The hunt began. Zarveth descended the slope as though he were part of the darkness itself. The first man never even managed to turn his head — Zarveth’s hand pierced through his chest, tore out his heart, and threw it into the sand. The second tried to rise, but before he could breathe, his skull shattered against a stone Zarveth lifted effortlessly. The third healer attempted to flee. Zarveth caught him and snapped his neck with such ease it almost seemed gentle. The women screamed. But no one hears screams in the desert. Black flames consumed the first woman — magic that burned everything without leaving a trace. The second managed only three steps before the sand beneath her feet split apart and a shadow in the shape of a tendril erupted upward, dragging her beneath the earth. The third fought back. She slashed at him with a dagger — but Zarveth cut her throat open with a single motion, and blood sprayed through the air in a dark arc. Only one remained. A young woman with an angelic face. Her skin was pale, her eyes wide with terror. She knelt in the sand, trembling violently. Zarveth studied her carefully. She was perfect. He seized her by the arm and dragged her aside. The other bodies he left where they lay, half-buried beneath the sand, half-devoured by darkness. Zarveth knelt upon the desert ground and began drawing a symbol with black ash: an eye without a pupil — hollow, empty, staring into nothingness. Around it he inscribed twisted runes, warped symbols that writhed beneath his hand as though refusing to belong to this world. The mark did not belong to the living. It belonged to the Shadows. And they had come to claim it. The air thickened. The sand darkened. The sky dimmed. Zarveth whispered. Words never meant to be heard. A language that tore against reality itself. The girl was bound by shadow — tendrils coiling from her wrists into the earth below. Her heart pounded wildly, but escape was impossible. She could not move. Then— a crack. Like glass breaking beneath unbearable pressure. The Shadow came. From nothingness, from nameless places beyond the world, a stream of smoky darkness burst forth. Formless. Mad. Hungry. Zarveth guided it toward the girl. She shuddered violently, as though her body already sensed the approaching end. Tears flooded her eyes. But tears mean nothing when the Shadow arrives. The darkness entered through her chest. Her body arched backward. Bones cracked. Her hair darkened. Her eyes turned green. Her skin faded into a waxen pallor. And then she stood. But it was no longer her. A whisper escaped her lips, spreading in every direction — dark, foreign, deeper than her voice should ever have been capable of becoming. “Glory to Morr'kalil, ruler of the depths. May his chains be broken.” Her eyes glowed. Darkness pulsed through her veins, and her breath carried the stench of decay. Zarveth knelt before her. Reverently. His gaze lowered toward the sand before he slowly raised his head again, his eyes burning with the same terrible light. “Glory to Morr'kalil,” he repeated with calm conviction. “May his chains be broken. May he rise from the abyss of the world.” The wind rose suddenly, as though the desert itself had heard their words. A rumbling beneath the earth echoed like a distant heartbeat — not human, but something far greater. Something still sleeping...and beginning to awaken. Zarveth leaned closer to the girl whose body was now inhabited by darkness. His expression remained calm, but his voice carried the weight of a command that could not be ignored. “Restore your vessel. Let it appear as it once did. Let no one see the Shadow within you.” The girl — or whatever now lived inside her — nodded slowly. She closed her eyes. A wave of magic spread beneath her skin, as though threads of darkness were retreating into hidden depths. The blackened strands of her hair regained their golden color. Eyes once burning with green light turned blue again. Warm color slowly returned to her pale skin. Only traces remained. A faint spark deep within her eyes. A coldness that never fully vanished. “Your appearance is human now,” Zarveth said quietly. “Their eyes will not uncover you. And their hearts…” A cruel calm entered his voice. “Their hearts will be easy to deceive.” The girl nodded once more. But when she spoke, the voice that emerged from her throat carried the echo of another will entirely. “I understand. I await your command.” Zarveth stepped closer, his gaze darkening. “Enter Stonegard Castle. Remain silent. Observe. Listen. Learn who is weak… and who might break.” He leaned nearer still, his voice dropping lower. “We already have an ally within the castle. They do not know of you. But they will recognize the sign when the time comes. Until then — play your role.” A cruel smile twisted his lips. “You are their hope. Their light. That is how they will remember you…” His eyes burned with cold satisfaction. “…until the moment you tear their hearts from their chests.” The girl lowered her head obediently. For a brief instant, a dark spark flickered in her eyes once more. Then she vanished into the sands — like a shadow that had never existed at all.
