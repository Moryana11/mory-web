Chapter VII. – A Fragment of time

As the sun climbed high above the horizon, its rays stopped warming the desert — they burned. Heat poured mercilessly from the sky, and the ground beneath them trembled as though every touch of sunlight were a wound. The air rippled in shimmering waves, heavy with tension. Nature itself had fallen into unnatural silence, as if the entire world were holding its breath. And yet… a low sound emerged. Deep. Quiet. Saturated. It sounded like an inhale far beneath their feet. Then the earth groaned. Not with the cry of magic, as one might expect. There was no glow of runes, no scent of burned herbs. Nothing that could be written down in books. The sound was raw. Human. And at the same time, inhuman. As though the earth itself were suffering. Not physically — but from memory. A memory of war. Not a war fought for land or crowns, but a war fought for ritual. A dark ceremony filled with pain and desperation, where countless human sacrifices were not a consequence of conflict, but the purpose itself. The earth remembered it. The screams. The blood soaking into the soil. And now, after years of silence, that memory was beginning to awaken. “Do you feel that?” Aaliyah asked quietly, as though afraid her voice might shatter something fragile. She stood one step ahead of the others, eyes narrowed against the sunlight, a thin sheen of sweat covering her brow — not from the heat, but from something deeper inside her. Her stomach twisted, and her heart beat unevenly like a warning bell before a storm. Rowena turned slowly and carefully. Her hair moved in a wind that should not have existed. “That’s not magic,” she said darkly. “It’s a Fragment of Time.” “A Fragment of Time?” Aaliyah asked in confusion. “Yes,” Rowena continued. “They’re fragments of memory the earth carries within itself like scars. Sometimes those memories awaken — they become tangible, alive. As though the world itself wishes to remind us what once happened… and warn us about what may come.” “But who could possibly—” Aaliyah began. Suddenly, the ground split apart with a horrifying crack — and darkness swallowed them whole. Within the black abyss, everything began to change. The shadows thickened, rippling like dense fog slowly taking shape into images of events long forgotten. The vision of the past blurred before them, colors fading into lifelessness. Time fractured. The landscape around them transformed, and suddenly they stood in the middle of the darkest era in history — the war against the fanatical cult, nearly five centuries earlier. It was an age when the land itself groaned beneath the suffering of its people. Every drop of blood, every scream of agony seeped into the earth like poison. The ground twisted in torment, cracking and trembling as though it felt every cruel act committed upon it. Nature itself begged for mercy. The world had nearly fallen to its knees before a god whose name was forbidden to be spoken. Morr’kalil. The dark god of blood, suffering, and death. He knew neither compassion nor mercy. In human suffering, he found profound satisfaction. He desired a world that would become nothing more than an endless wound that could never heal. His followers believed the world was irreparably corrupted and that only through suffering could it be purified. Morr’kalil was their truth, their law, their savior within the flames. He dwelled within the Darkness — an empty realm beyond death where lost souls drowned within the fog of oblivion. He fed upon them. Their pain sustained him. But he wanted more. He wanted the living. He wanted to transform the entire world into a burning temple of agony. His followers swept across the lands like a plague. They searched for four sacred places where the boundary between this world and Morr’kalil’s realm was thinnest, so they could offer sacrifices there. It did not matter who was sacrificed. Morr’kalil did not choose. Everyone was suitable. Anyone who possessed a tongue capable of screaming for help and a body capable of bleeding. The victims were dragged toward the altars. Chains bound their hands while their heads were forced against the ground. They screamed, begged for mercy, gasped desperately for breath while terror twisted their faces. And around them… the crowd danced. The fanatics stood in circles surrounding the sacrifices, their faces warped by twisted madness and religious fanaticism that consumed every part of their being. Around their necks hung heavy amulets — cold metallic disks with dark reflective surfaces, engraved with a single eyeless eye that seemed to follow every movement with merciless emptiness. Their chanting sounded almost ecstatic. A prayer woven from pain and destruction, born from their obsessive belief that every cut and every drop of blood was a sacred ritual cleansing the world. They laughed cruelly, with a fury capable of tearing apart the soul itself and crushing the heart beneath the weight of their corrupted devotion. Animalistic pupils danced within their widened eyes, burning with fanatic zeal and unwavering faith in Morr’kalil as the only true savior and judge. The bodies of the sacrifices were mutilated with such precision that it appeared almost… beautiful. But it was not beauty meant for the world of humans. It was beauty for Morr’kalil. The shadows moved around them. The soil was red. The air burned. And the silence between the screams carried the weight of stone. Nothing ever grew in those places again. Magic fractured there. Light avoided the ground itself. The air tasted like ash. And time became lost. And in the age of greatest darkness, when three rituals had already been completed and the fourth was meant to become the crown of Morr’kalil’s return, a desperate act was born. The Resistance. The three kingdoms of the continent of Solisium — Laslan, Stonegard, and Talandre — united their forces for the first time in history, though each understood the darkness differently. They were not bound together by hope, but by desperation. The desperation born from knowing that if they failed, the world would be consumed by endless suffering. It was a war unlike any before it. Not a struggle for land, crowns, or faith. It was a battle for the soul of the world itself — for the right to sunlight untouched by pain, for love untouched by ruin, for death untouched by the mockery of the dark god Morr’kalil. Battles raged across endless plains, through labyrinths of underground tunnels, upon storm-torn seas, and high in the skies. Ancient cities that had witnessed forgotten ages burned beneath waves of fire. Tens of thousands of warriors — knights, mages, and mercenaries alike — perished in blood among smoke, shadows, and screams. And at last came the final battle within the Swamp of Silence, a place where the currents of time, memory, and will converged, where the boundary between worlds was thin as paper itself. There, upon the threshold of reality, the fate of all living things was decided. The army of the Resistance drove back Morr’kalil’s final followers as they advanced like manifestations of death itself. Then came the sealing ritual — a dangerous and immensely complex ceremony whose power was meant to force the dark god back into the abyss from which he had risen. The gate between the world and the Darkness closed. But time itself fractured within the Swamp of Silence. Those who survived began seeing things never meant to be seen. Memory twisted and rewrote itself. Past and present bled together in a horrifying dance. The line between reality and madness dissolved. But Morr’kalil…never forgot. The vision slowly faded and dissolved like mist before yielding to another. This time, Rowena, Aaliyah, and James found themselves standing within an ancient ruined temple. Broken pillars surrounded them, mold crept across the walls, and shadows stretched in unnatural patterns. The air felt heavy and stale. And in the center of that silent ruin stood a mirror. The Mirror of Shadows. Tall. Ancient. Its frame was covered in carved symbols pulsing faintly with dark light. The surface of the glass was not smooth but rippled and shifted constantly, as though the mirror itself were breathing. And those who looked into it did not see only their reflection. They saw something more. The air surrounding it was cold and yet unbearably heavy, as though the mirror devoured hope itself and replaced it with doubt. “That’s not an ordinary mirror,” Rowena whispered. Her voice sounded strange — muffled, as though something were suppressing it. “It shows not only what is and what was… but what could be. Or what we fear most.” Aaliyah and James remained silent. Slowly, they stepped closer, their footsteps echoing through the empty hall like drops of water striking stone. They stopped beside Rowena. And then they looked. In the mirror, Rowena saw herself — and yet it was not truly her. The reflection was distorted and foreign, like some darker, lost fragment of her soul. Her eyes were empty of light, her hair matted against her face, her robes torn apart, her hands covered in blood. She sat alone within a ruined city surrounded by bodies — not enemies, but allies. In her arms lay her parents, motionless, burned, their eyes vacant and lifeless. Rowena screamed, but no sound emerged. She clutched their bodies desperately, trying to heal them, but the magic crumbled between her fingers like dust. “No… no…” she whispered aloud. Her hand shot toward the mirror — but the glass remained cold and impenetrable. Aaliyah stood at the edge of a vast abyss within the mirror’s reflection. Freezing wind lashed against her face, and darkness filled her eyes — darkness woven from pain, fear, and helplessness. Her face was pale and rigid, as though she had long forgotten how to smile. Every line of her expression carried a silent plea for relief — the desire to jump, to end the suffering, to finally escape the unbearable weight devouring her from within piece by piece. The mirror showed her the embodiment of her deepest fear — the fear of love that brought only pain instead of joy. She feared that if she opened herself again, if she allowed herself to feel again… she would end up shattered exactly as before. That James could become another wound. Another William. Aaliyah staggered back a step as though the mirror had burned her. Her eyes widened, and her lips trembled. “That’s not me…” she whispered, even though somewhere deep inside she knew it was. The part of herself she had spent so long trying to bury. She swallowed hard and pressed a hand against her chest, as though trying to hold together a heart beating too hard, too painfully. “I don’t want to fall apart again…” she breathed shakily. “I don’t want to love again just to lose everything again.” But the mirror remained silent. And her reflection remained there — alone, broken, ready to jump. James stood before the mirror. His reflection revealed a dark world born from unreturned love and loss. Within the vision, he stood inside an empty hall where the light itself was suffocated by thick shadows. At the center of that darkness knelt Aaliyah. Her body trembled violently, eyes reddened from crying, her face pale as ash. Strands of red hair hung across her face, hiding part of her expression, yet not enough to conceal the devastation within her. Above her stood William with a cruel, amused smile. He was enjoying her suffering. James closed his eyes. And when he opened them again, the vision had changed. An empty hall. Aaliyah lying motionless upon the floor. Dead. She had taken poison. Within the silence of the hall, her death screamed louder than words ever could. James closed his eyes a second time, and the mirror shifted once more. William now lay dead upon the ground. His skin was pale, almost gray with ash, his lips blue. Blood mixed with foam slowly trickled from his mouth. James knew exactly how William had died. He recognized death by poison instantly. A poison he himself had used to kill many times before. Quick. Silent. Invisible. James stood above William’s corpse, eyes empty and utterly without remorse. He had done it because he could not endure losing Aaliyah. Because he could never truly have her. Because she had never chosen him. James remained standing before the mirror, his eyes steady and resolute. He was not frightened by what he had seen. He understood now how deep his love for Aaliyah truly was — and how far he would be willing to go in order to protect her. The mirror darkened. Its carvings faded. The room fell into oppressive silence. But the vision was not over. They still stood within the ruined temple. Their breathing became uneven, as though the air itself had thickened into lead. And then it happened — as if the boundary between vision and reality had cracked apart. The images shown by the mirror did not vanish. Instead, they sank into them. Aaliyah and Rowena looked different now. Not as they truly were. But as the mirror had revealed them. Rowena could barely remain standing. The light had vanished from her eyes, leaving them hollow, darkened, lifeless — as though the vision still echoed within them. Her hair hung heavily against her face, damp with sweat and blood. Fresh crimson drops slid downward in a slow rhythm that mixed with the fading traces of magic. Her robes, once dignified, were torn apart and soaked with dust and the unbearable weight of horrors she could not even name. Beside her, Aaliyah looked almost unreal. Her face had become so pale she resembled a ghost of herself. Dark circles shadowed her eyes. Her red hair hung loose and tangled, damp from tears. Her robes were wrinkled and clung loosely to her body. Meanwhile, from the cracked mirror emerged a faint shadow. It was not fully tangible. More like cold mist slowly spreading into the space around them. Suffocating. Relentless. A memory refusing to disappear even though it should have been forgotten long ago. Rowena trembled violently and clutched her arms against her chest as though fighting pain crushing her heart. “We’re trapped here,” she whispered, her voice shaking. “This Fragment of Time has trapped us tightly, like deadly claws,” Rowena whispered. “Time doesn’t flow here. Everything around us is frozen inside an endless moment — a prison slowly tearing us apart piece by piece.” Aaliyah pressed her palms against her temples, struggling to suppress the rising panic. “We need to find a weakness,” she said shakily. “A crack in this endless grip.” James stood silently, his cold eyes following the mist spreading around them. “Time here isn’t merely frozen,” he finally said, his voice carrying no room for doubt. “Its flow has been shattered. A way out must exist somewhere between the fragments.” The shadow emerging from the broken mirror thickened further. The swirling mist drew inward, yet the crushing weight of the trap remained. “This is a battle against time itself,” James said quietly. “And we have to be faster than it.”
