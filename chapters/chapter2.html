Chapter II. – Sands of the Moonlight Desert

Several weeks had passed since they left the ruins of Turayne behind, and their journey had eventually led them into the unforgiving Moonlight Desert. The sun hung in the sky like a blazing disc. Sand cracked and burned beneath their feet. Though the land appeared empty at first glance, every howl of wind concealed movement — snakes, sand thieves, and massive sandworms lurking beneath the dunes. Rowena sweated beneath her dark blue robes, the fabric having long since lost its former sheen. The water in her flask was running low. Her magic protected her from the heat, but even that did not possess endless reserves. Still, she said nothing. She simply kept walking. Beside her, Aaliyah bounded across the dunes as though she did not feel exhaustion at all. “Hey, if we happen to find desert baths somewhere around here, I’m absolutely signing up for a sand wrap and cactus therapy!” she announced enthusiastically just as they came across the skeleton of a giant scorpion. “Though I’d really prefer to skip the scorpion massage.” Rowena merely laughed. When night finally came and the moon illuminated the dunes like silver waves, they made camp between two rocky outcrops. Aaliyah started a fire with the flick of a finger, while Rowena brewed tea from desert herbs she had gathered earlier. They sat beside one another in silence. Around them, only the wind occasionally howled through the dunes. “You know most people go mad out here within three days?” Aaliyah asked, staring into the flames. “Because they hear voices,” Rowena answered quietly. “Memories the desert brings back to life. It has a memory of its own.” Aaliyah smiled faintly. “Do you hear anything?” Rowena hesitated. Then she nodded. “I hear myself. All my doubts. Every moment when I could have saved someone and failed to reach them in time.” “Those aren’t the desert’s voices,” Aaliyah said, looking at her. “That’s your conscience. And honestly, yours is so huge you could probably smother an entire continent with it.” Rowena sighed softly. “And you? Do you hear anything?” Aaliyah fell silent. But the silence did not last long. Something beneath the sand began to rise beneath their feet — large, heavy, covered in scales. Rowena reacted instantly, spreading a protective barrier around them. From the ground emerged a Sand Lizard, a monstrous creature with a maw full of needle-like teeth and eyes like two black pearls. The moment its massive body burst free from the dunes, it lunged forward violently. Its jaws opened wide, and with a terrifying hiss, it slammed directly into the glowing shield. A dull impact echoed through the night, like stone striking metal. The magical barrier flared brightly, and a wave of force hurled the beast backward into the sand. The lizard hissed furiously, claws tearing through the dunes as it rose again. Rowena clenched her fingers, her gaze sharpening. “Now!” she shouted. The shield around them faded, dissolving into golden sparks. Aaliyah was already moving. She leapt forward, her burning staff gripped tightly in her hand. With one sharp motion, she conjured a pulsing sphere of fire before her — bright and blazing like a miniature sun. The fireball shot forward, scorching the air around it. But the Sand Lizard was faster than either of them expected. At the last instant, it twisted violently to the side, its heavy body digging into the sand as the blazing projectile tore past its head. The explosion scattered sand in every direction. The beast roared again and charged without hesitation. This time, it went straight for Aaliyah. Its jaws snapped through the air, rows of teeth gleaming like needles ready to rip flesh apart. Aaliyah dodged aside, her flaming staff trailing sparks. “Stupid lizard,” she hissed. She moved fast. With a single sharp flick of her wrist, she gathered magic into her palm once more. The flames around her spiraled inward, twisting into another fireball within a heartbeat. She unleashed it directly at the beast. The Sand Lizard tried to dodge again, but this time it was too late. The fireball struck it squarely in the head. A violent explosion cracked through the desert night, and flames poured across its scales like a wave of molten heat. The creature shrieked in agony. Its body convulsed wildly, its tail thrashing across the sand while a ragged screech burst from its throat. The scorching air trembled as its scales blackened and split beneath the heat. Then the lizard collapsed. Burned alive. The magical flames slowly faded, thinning into drifting ribbons of smoke. The air remained heavy and blistering hot, thick with the stench of burning flesh. Rowena and Aaliyah stood several steps away from the dead beast, breathing hard. Neither of them took their eyes off it. The massive body lay motionless in the sand, but both women remained tense and ready, waiting to see if it would move again. Several long seconds passed. Nothing. Only the wind stirred the sand gently around the creature’s charred scales. Once it became clear the beast would not rise again, the tension slowly drained from their bodies. The flames surrounding Aaliyah finally died out completely, and her shoulders relaxed ever so slightly. Rowena immediately stepped closer and swept a quick, practiced glance over her from head to toe. She gently touched Aaliyah’s arm, as though checking for pain or hidden injuries. “Are you alright?” she asked quietly. Aaliyah nodded, her breathing still uneven but her gaze steady. “Yeah. Not even a scratch. I’m starting to think you’re cursed, Rowena,” she said with a laugh. “Ever since I met you, I haven’t had a single peaceful day.” “Maybe I am,” Rowena replied. “But you’re insane for staying.” Aaliyah smiled faintly. “Or maybe I’ve just gotten used to the chaos.” When they sat back down beside the dying fire, neither of them spoke. Far beyond the dunes, movement appeared within the darkness — a shadow slowly shifting beneath the moonlight. And with it came a strange, heavy rhythm. Footsteps. “That’s not an animal,” Aaliyah said quietly. Rowena nodded. “No. It’s something else.” Without another word, they rose to their feet and silently watched the shadow slowly drawing closer.
