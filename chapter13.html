Chapter XIII. – Sand and Silver

Dawn was breaking when they finally arrived at the Sanctuary Oasis. Aaliyah wiped dust from her forehead and raised a sweaty hand to shield her eyes. From afar, the place had looked like a mirage — a dream woven from palm trees, shimmering water, and stone towers. But as they descended the winding path between the cliffs, the dream slowly became reality. The air was still dry, yet far cooler than in the wasteland beyond. The surrounding cliffs offered shelter from the burning heat. A gentle breeze drifted along the stone road, carrying with it the scent of date palms, smoke, and spices. Voices murmured in the distance alongside the ringing of metal and the calls of caravan traders. James walked at the front, gaze fixed ahead. One hand remained firmly on his weapon while the other was offered to Rowena, who stumbled slightly along the gravelly slope. Aaliyah walked at his other side, watching the life that pulsed through the oasis. When they reached the entrance, two local guards wrapped in colorful scarves studied them from afar but made no move to stop them. Foreign travelers were common in the oasis, so long as they did not disturb its balance. And the three of them looked more exhausted than dangerous — though their eyes suggested they carried far more than the dust of the road. “Water…” Rowena whispered when she caught sight of the lake surrounded by the shade of a fig tree. Her voice sounded almost reverent. “And maybe a bed,” Aaliyah added tiredly, though with a faint smile. They sat down on the soft sand near the edge of the marketplace. Around them flowed a river of people — men in turbans, women in flowing garments of light fabric, barefoot children darting swiftly between the stalls. James nodded toward a cluster of tents. “I’ll see if they have a free place for us.” As James disappeared into the crowd of merchants, Aaliyah and Rowena watched the vibrant life of the Sanctuary Oasis. For Aaliyah, this was not an unfamiliar place — she had visited many times before. She had always been drawn to the bustling market spread between the cliffs, where everything from fragrant resins to strange half-magical creatures was sold. The scent of date palms and spices was deeply familiar to her, and the murmur of the marketplace could calm her even after long days crossing the wasteland. Suddenly, a man in a blue turban addressed them. He had piercing dark brown eyes and a firm yet friendly voice. “Aaliyah? It’s been a long time since I’ve seen you here.” Rowena looked at him curiously but said nothing. She merely tilted her head slightly, studying both of their faces more closely while allowing Aaliyah to speak. Aaliyah gave a faint nod. “Yes, I was away. For a long time. Longer than I originally intended.” Her voice was steady, yet warm. It carried the weight of memories. “But I never stopped thinking about the Sanctuary Oasis.” She smiled softly, though more to herself than to anyone else. “When I was a child, my father used to take me to the Spring of Knowledge,” she said quietly. “He said that water and memory became one there, and that some people could hear its voice.” She paused for a moment, her gaze fixed on the lake shimmering silver between the palms. “But neither of us ever heard it,” she added. “We only imagined it.” She took a slow breath. “But fire… that one speaks. Its voice is fierce, sometimes angry, sometimes almost gentle. It never waits. It simply speaks — and forces you to listen.” Rowena gave her a brief, thoughtful glance but said nothing. She merely adjusted her clothing. “Fire never lies,” the man in the blue turban said seriously. “It only says what people are too afraid to speak aloud. Just like Aaliyah.” Then he smiled faintly and gestured toward Rowena. “I’m Harim,” he introduced himself warmly. “It’s good to meet you.” Aaliyah smiled slightly before adding, “Harim is one of the people who keeps order in the oasis and helps those who come here. You can trust him.” Harim sat down beside the women, and together they waited for James to return. From time to time, his eyes scanned the surroundings — alert and watchful, yet calm. When James finally appeared, he approached them and gave Harim a nod. “Come with me, I’ve found a place for you,” Harim said firmly, leading them toward one of the larger tents near the edge of the oasis. “You’ll spend the night here. It’s safe and well guarded. You need to regain your strength.” The women followed him, feeling the exhaustion of the desert heat and dust slowly begin to fade from their bodies. James walked closely behind them. The three of them knew that, for as long as they remained within the Sanctuary Oasis, they would have a brief moment of peace.
