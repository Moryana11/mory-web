Chapter III. – Fire and shadow

Flashback from Aaliyah’s point of view Golden light from the setting sun fell across the encampment, where the euphoria of another victorious expedition slowly dissolved into dust and exhausted stares. Aaliyah sat on a stone with her legs crossed, her head resting in her hands. Her robes were scorched from magic, her arms weary, and her heart beating faster than it should have been. Behind her, she heard a voice she knew far too well. “You overdid it again, Red. That’s what happens when you try to save the whole world by yourself.” Aaliyah smiled even though she did not want to. William. Forever sarcastic and charming. He could make the entire unit laugh even in the darkest moments. His touch was warm, his words always certain. He seemed to have an answer for everything. He was a wonderful friend. And a very selfish lover. He approached her still wearing his armor, shield strapped across his back and sword hanging at his side. His short brown hair was slightly tousled, neatly trimmed brown beard framing a handsome sun-touched face with a smile that looked both inviting — and perhaps faintly mocking. Aaliyah turned away and muttered dryly, “Well, if I hadn’t used my magic at full strength, we’d probably all be dead right now… so thanks for the concern, hero.” William laughed. “I’m glad you’re so selfless… must’ve exhausted you, huh?” he said, tilting his head teasingly. Then he sat beside her, close enough for her to feel the heat radiating from him. Leaning toward her ear, he lowered his voice. “I hope you saved a little energy… for tonight.” Aaliyah raised an eyebrow and frowned, yet a faint smile still spread across her face. That was how things were between them for several months — teasing, light flirtation, passionate nights, and tension that never truly faded. And then one day she woke up… …and he was gone. No goodbye. No explanation. Only a short note left on the pillow beside her. I’m done with you. Aaliyah picked it up, her hands trembling. The words were brief, cold — but the pain they caused burned hot and endless. Her heart pounded so hard she felt as though she could hear every beat inside her skull. Instinctively, she threw herself out of bed and rushed toward his tent, hoping he would be there — hoping this was all some cruel joke. But the tent was empty. All of his belongings were gone. Aaliyah stood before the vacant tent for a long time. Tears filled her eyes, and pain burned inside her chest like a fire that could never be extinguished. And from the distance, James watched her. He leaned against a small rock hidden within the shadows. The warrior remained silent, but his eyes spoke enough. Aaliyah noticed him immediately and understood that he had seen everything — how she laughed, how she loved, and how she fell apart. That evening, while she sat alone beside the fire, James quietly approached her. He lowered himself beside her and, after a long silence, finally spoke. “You deserve more than being someone’s trophy.” Aaliyah looked at him carefully. There was something different in his gaze. Not pity. But admiration. Respect. And something quiet that pierced deeper than William’s betrayal ever had. “What do you want, James?” she asked. “Reassurance that you’re better than him?” The anger burst into her voice before she could stop it. She knew she was lashing out at the wrong person. None of this was his fault. It never had been. But he simply sat there. Calm. Honest. And that irritated her more than anything else. He shook his head. “I want… to protect you. Not like some savior. Just as someone who sees you for who you are. Not because of what you do for everyone else.” His words caught her off guard. For a moment, she only stared at him in silence. She realized what he was truly offering her. Genuine feelings. “Don’t say that, James… Please,” she whispered. “I want you to know I’m not expecting anything. But… I’ve spent a long time watching you burn yourself away for other people. And if anyone should be the one protecting you… I’d want it to be me,” he answered quietly. At that moment, her heart tightened painfully, because she knew that if she allowed herself to trust him… …he could betray her too. James fell silent for a moment before lowering his gaze. “I’m not pressuring you,” he added softly. “You don’t have to answer me. You don’t have to decide today. Or tomorrow.” He lifted his eyes to hers, calm and without expectation. “Just think about it.” Then he stepped back. Only one step. As though that single movement closed everything that had passed between them. He turned and disappeared into the night, leaving her beside the fire — surrounded by warmth, the crackling flames, and all the thoughts burning inside her chest. Two days later, they stood by the gates of Vienta, where the unit finally parted ways. “Forgive me, James…” she said softly. He nodded. No anger. No pressure. “I’ll come back. When you want me to.” And then he left. For two years, Aaliyah gathered the shattered pieces of her heart and tried to put them back together. During the day, she ate, fought, and smiled — the perfect illusion she showed the world. But the nights were her ruin. When silence settled around her and her body finally rested upon the bed, her mind filled with painful memories of the two men who refused to leave her in peace. The first man had been like a magnet, drawing everyone around him closer. Dominant. Unshakable in his presence. He controlled her with a gaze that burned hotter than the desert sun. His touch could ignite a fire inside her so fierce that she forgot the existence of everything else. In those moments, it felt as though the world belonged to them, as though she herself had become his obsession, his favorite game. But it had all been an illusion that vanished with the dawn. His passion was only a fleeting flame that consumed everything standing in its path, leaving nothing behind but ash. Aaliyah often felt like a toy he had discarded the moment she stopped entertaining him. His words were sharp as blades, careless and cruel. He was a man who played with emotions as though they were objects to manipulate, and no one — not even her — was more than a tool to satisfy whatever desire ruled him in the moment. And yet, despite driving her to the edge of despair, he left behind a mark that still burned within he — pain, and the memory of moments when passion had been so overwhelming it stole the breath from her lungs. The second man in her life was the very opposite of a storm. He was quiet as twilight creeping across the land. Full of secrets, silent and deep like a well Aaliyah had long been afraid to look into. He rarely spoke — sometimes remaining silent for hours, simply watching her with a gaze that held not only desire, but something far more dangerous. Something sincere. Though restrained, every gesture he made quietly screamed tenderness. He handed her water before she could ask for it. Gave her his blanket even when he himself must have been cold. He never pushed her when she lacked the strength to speak. And within that restraint… within his gentle touch that demanded nothing in return… within his silence, which was never empty but full of meaning… Aaliyah understood for the first time what it meant to be wanted not for her body — but for her soul.
