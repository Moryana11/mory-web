Chapter XIX. – The road to stonegard castle

The sand beneath their feet still held the coolness of the night, but the sun was already climbing higher into the sky, its sharp rays beginning to burn against their skin. The air grew warmer and heavier, carrying with it the dusty scent of the desert. The three of them walked southward, toward the distant outline of Stonegard Castle, which loomed on the horizon like a dark shadow against the bright blue sky. Rowena walked at the front, the orb inside her satchel pulsing faintly with silver light, as though breathing in rhythm with her heart. Every step she took was careful yet steady, as if an invisible thread of fate were guiding her directly toward the place where she would finally face the truth she sought. James walked behind them, his eyes sharp and alert, constantly scanning their surroundings, prepared to react to the slightest movement or sign of danger. Aaliyah walked between them, silent and focused, her mind tangled in questions she still lacked the courage to speak aloud — questions that tightened painfully around her heart. “Do you feel it?” Rowena asked quietly, her voice barely audible. “The air is changing. Something is close. Something watching us.” James nodded, his eyes narrowing. “It’s like darkness waiting around the corner. Not here yet… but waiting for the right moment to strike.” Suddenly, a muffled rustling sound echoed across the sand. The three of them froze instantly, their eyes darting across the landscape. Dark whirlwinds slowly began rising from the desert floor, creeping toward them with the wind as though the earth itself had turned against them. Rowena quickly raised the orb, which erupted with brilliant white light. Its radiance dissolved parts of the dark spirals the way the first rays of dawn melt ice. “Morr’kalil is already searching for us,” Aaliyah whispered, breathing heavily. James drew his spear, the blade gleaming beneath the morning sun. “We keep moving. No hesitation.” Then a figure emerged from the vortex of darkness — tall, shadowed, wrapped in living blackness. Golden eyes burned like smoldering embers. Black armor covered in spikes reflected no light, while a sword and spear rested firmly in his hands. Zarveth. The dark knight whose arrival meant only one thing — ruin and chaos. Without hesitation, the three of them moved into a defensive formation. The air around them thickened with tension and heat, while the sand beneath their feet seemed alive, absorbing the magic radiating from their bodies. James gripped his spear tightly, his movements focused and precise. With every thrust, he tried to pierce the darkness surrounding Zarveth, delivering sharp and calculated strikes. But James quickly realized that the heavy, slower weapon would not help him against an opponent so powerful and unnervingly fast. One of his strikes was effortlessly blocked by Zarveth, who violently knocked the spear aside, forcing James to step back quickly to avoid losing his balance. In one swift motion, he abandoned the spear and drew two concealed daggers, their blades glistening with a poisonous coating. With the daggers in hand, he could move faster — weaving elegantly between attacks and delivering deadly strikes toward the weak points of his enemy’s armor. Aaliyah gripped her long magical staff tightly, its surface covered in glowing runes pulsing with orange-red light. Every movement she made unleashed streams of fire that spiraled around her like living serpents, twisting and writhing through the air with wild energy. These creatures of flame devoured the darkness without mercy. Rowena stood a step behind them, holding an open spellbook and a short wand. Her magic was quiet, calm, and unbelievably powerful. Pearlescent light radiated from her touch, forming a protective veil around her companions, healing their wounds, easing their pain, and strengthening both mind and body so they could continue resisting the evil before them. The battle was turning against them. James, Aaliyah, and Rowena were exhausted. Their attacks tore through parts of Zarveth’s defenses, but none were enough to land a fatal blow. His dark magic consumed the fire spells and even the poison coating James’s blades, while his sinister laughter echoed across the desert like the sound of ruin itself. The three of them were beginning to collapse. Their breathing grew ragged, their magic weakened, and their movements slowed with exhaustion. Then Rowena suddenly felt warmth at her side. The orb inside her satchel glowed and radiated heat, reacting to the presence of Zarveth’s darkness. Without hesitation, she seized it and raised it toward him. The light intensified, flooding the battlefield, and with a low humming sound, a translucent shield formed around the three companions. Zarveth stopped abruptly. His golden eyes locked not onto the shield… but onto Rowena herself. “So you are the one carrying it,” he said coldly. Both contempt and unease echoed within his voice. “And you’ve even learned how to awaken it.” With a sudden burst of fury, he lunged forward, channeling all of his strength against the shield as though he intended to shatter not only the barrier, but the orb itself within her hands. The impact struck with terrifying force. The shield trembled violently, its light flickering dangerously. Zarveth’s armor shook with every collision, his movements heavy yet filled with dreadful power. The sword and spear danced in his grasp like extensions of a dark will determined to destroy all living things. Rowena gasped sharply. The orb grew heavier within her hands, its glow pulsing unevenly. The magic inside it writhed violently, as though trying to break free. Fine, dark-silver cracks spread across the smooth surface of the shield, and with a muffled thunderous sound, the barrier shattered. The orb in Rowena’s hands glowed faintly one final time — and then went dark. Its surface dimmed and turned cold. The force of the impact drove them to their knees. James hit the ground hard, bracing himself with one hand while the daggers trembled in his fingers. Aaliyah collapsed beside him, her staff falling helplessly into the sand, its flames long extinguished. Rowena knelt behind them, staring blankly at the lifeless orb, her breathing ragged, her body drained of every last ounce of strength. Zarveth straightened slowly. The darkness surrounding him fell silent, as though sensing the approaching end. He raised his weapon for a slow, certain, fatal strike. And at that very moment — when everything seemed lost — a distant rumbling echoed across the desert. Aaliyah gasped. “Do you hear that?” she whispered. All of them froze. The thunder grew louder. Closer. Silhouettes appeared above the dunes — dark riders whose armor gleamed beneath the morning light like flashes of starlight across steel. Their horses tore through the sand like crashing waves, and the clatter of their weapons rang across the desert like the sound of hope itself. “They’re…” Aaliyah breathed, before shouting with all the strength she had left, her voice desperate and urgent: “Help! We’re here! Help!” The commander of the knights — a massive man clad in steel armor with a cloak the color of the night sky — raised his sword and gave the order to charge. Power surged behind him. His riders stormed forward like a wave of light crashing into battle. Zarveth reacted instantly. His eyes darkened as he turned toward the approaching cavalry with a hiss. That single moment of distraction was enough. James moved. Silently, swiftly, he closed the distance. The daggers flew. One buried itself deep into Zarveth’s face, the second following a fraction of a second later. Metal struck inhuman flesh. Zarveth roared as his head jerked violently aside, dark blood erupting from the black wound. He staggered. The darkness within him faltered, twisting violently as though the force controlling him had briefly lost its direction. A burst of black magic exploded from his mouth, only to disintegrate into scraps of shadow beneath the light of the approaching knights. His golden eyes dimmed, narrowing into thin slits burning with hatred. “This is not over…” he rasped, his voice deep, hissing, overflowing with malice. “I will return. And when I do… there will be no one left to stop me.” With one final hiss, he retreated into the darkness — wounded and humiliated. His body dissolved into streams of black mist that seeped into the sand, as though the desert itself were devouring him. The air grew heavier after his departure, as if it understood that the silence left behind was not peace… but the stillness before a far greater storm. James, Aaliyah, and Rowena remained standing — soaked in sweat, breathless, wounded, yet alive. The knights of the Laslan Allied Resistance arrived only seconds later. Their horses came to a sharp halt as their eyes swept across the battlefield, prepared to confront the enemy. But Zarveth was already gone. James dropped to one knee, the daggers still clenched tightly in his hands, ragged breaths tearing from his chest. “He didn’t vanish,” he muttered. “He retreated. He realized he couldn’t win… not yet.” Aaliyah remained silent. Her staff slowly cooled in her grasp, its flames now reduced to glowing embers flickering faintly between the runes. Rowena, still clutching her book tightly, gave a faint nod, her gaze fixed on the place where Zarveth had disappeared. “I felt it,” she whispered. “Something inside him… as though it was trying to break free. He isn’t complete yet. But once he is…” She never finished the sentence. None of them needed her to. In the silence that followed the battle lingered something tense and dreadful — a promise that the darkness would rise again. And next time, it would return far stronger.
