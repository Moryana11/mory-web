Chapter XX. – Ash upon the skin

The sun climbed higher into the sky, its light falling across the battlefield where the dust had not yet settled. The air was thick with the scent of ash, magic, and iron. The knights of the Laslan Allied Resistance dismounted from their horses, their heavy footsteps leaving deep marks in the sand. Some rushed immediately toward the three figures standing within the center of the scorched circle, while others cautiously searched the surrounding dunes, prepared for another attack. The commander of the knights, a tall scarred man with blond hair, a full beard, and blue eyes, approached them with a mixture of respect and unease. His armor was coated in dust, his swords once again sheathed across his back. “We received a message. Harim’s hawk reached our camp shortly after dawn. We left immediately.” Aaliyah nodded, her eyes fixed on the horizon where the sand still trembled faintly. “You arrived just in time. Another moment, and Zarveth would have killed us.” Rowena knelt nearby. The orb in her hand glowed dimly, as though it too was recovering from exhaustion. Sweat shimmered across her forehead, mixed with dust and ash clinging to her skin like a reminder of the battle. “He lost power… but not his resolve,” she murmured without looking up. “He’ll return. And next time, he won’t come alone.” James sat beside her, his daggers now put away, his abandoned spear left somewhere far behind them. His eyes remained sharp, yet a heavy burden weighed upon him — the realization that even three against one had not been enough. “He was faster than anything I’ve ever fought,” he admitted quietly. “And that still wasn’t his full strength.” The knights began distributing supplies — water, bandages, healing elixirs. A young squire offered Rowena his hand, but she only shook her head softly and rose on her own. Her movements were unsteady, yet her eyes remained firm. “We need to reach Stonegard Castle,” she said quietly, almost to herself. “Before something worse than Zarveth emerges from the darkness.” “If the Seal is breaking, then this was only the first strike,” Aaliyah agreed while silently wiping blood from her fingers. Her magical staff rested beside her, the runes along its surface still glowing faintly, as though the weapon itself remembered the fire it had unleashed. James rose to his feet and helped Aaliyah stand. “From now on, we stay quiet while traveling. The darkness following us may hear more than we realize.” When the three of them continued onward once again — now beneath the protection of the knights and riding the horses they had been given — their journey became faster. And high above them, beyond the clouds, a lone hawk flew back toward the Sanctuary Oasis, carrying news of what had happened.
