Chapter II. – The Sands of the Moonlight Desert

Several weeks had passed since they left the ruins of Turayne behind, and their journey had eventually led them into the unforgiving Moonlight Desert. The sun hung in the sky like a blazing furnace. Sand cracked and burned beneath their feet. Though the landscape appeared empty, every gust of wind concealed movement — snakes, sand thieves, and enormous sandworms. Rowena sweated beneath her dark blue robe, the fabric having long since lost its former sheen. The water in her flask was running low. Her magic protected her from the heat, but even that power was not endless. Still, she said nothing. She kept walking. Beside her, Aaliyah bounded across the dunes as though exhaustion did not exist. “Hey, if we happen to find desert baths somewhere out here, I’m absolutely signing up for a sand wrap and cactus therapy!” she announced enthusiastically just as they passed the skeleton of a gigantic scorpion. “Though I’m definitely refusing the scorpion massage.” Rowena only laughed softly. When night finally came and the moon illuminated the dunes like silver waves, they made camp between two rocky outcrops. Aaliyah lit a fire with the flick of a finger while Rowena prepared tea from desert herbs she had gathered earlier. They sat side by side. Silence surrounded them, broken only occasionally by the howl of the wind. “You know most people go insane out here within three days?” Aaliyah asked, staring into the flames. “Because they hear voices,” Rowena answered quietly. “Memories the desert awakens. It has a memory of its own.” Aaliyah smiled faintly. “Do you hear anything?” Rowena hesitated. Then she nodded. “I hear myself. All my doubts. Every moment when I could have saved someone and failed to do it in time.” “That’s not the desert talking,” Aaliyah said, looking at her. “That’s your conscience. And it’s so huge you could probably smother an entire continent with it.” Rowena sighed softly. “And you? Do you hear anything?” Aaliyah fell silent. But the silence did not last long. Something began rising from the sand beneath their feet — large, heavy, covered in scales. Rowena reacted instantly, spreading a protective barrier around them. From the ground emerged a Sand Lizard, a monstrous creature with a maw full of needle-like teeth and eyes like two black pearls. The moment its body fully burst from the sand, it lunged forward violently. Its jaws opened wide, and with a horrifying hiss it slammed directly into the glowing shield. A deep impact rang out, like stone crashing against metal. The magical barrier flared brightly, and the wave of energy hurled the beast backward into the sand. The lizard hissed furiously, claws tearing through the dunes as it rose again. Rowena clenched her fingers, her gaze sharpening. “Now!” she shouted. The shield around them dimmed and dissolved into golden sparks. Aaliyah was already moving. She leapt forward, flaming staff gripped tightly in her hand. With one swift motion, she conjured a pulsing sphere of fire before her, bright and blazing like a miniature sun. The fireball shot forward, heating the air around it. But the Sand Lizard was faster than expected. At the last second, it twisted violently aside, its massive body diving into the sand while the fiery projectile streaked past its head. The explosion scattered sand in every direction. The beast roared again and charged without hesitation. This time, straight at Aaliyah. Its jaws snapped through the air, rows of teeth gleaming like needles ready to tear flesh apart. Aaliyah dodged aside, staff blazing in her hand. “Stupid lizard,” she hissed. She was fast. With one sharp flick of her wrist, she gathered magic into her palm once more. Fire spiraled around her, twisting into a vortex before condensing within a heartbeat into another blazing sphere. She launched it directly at the creature. The Sand Lizard tried to evade again, but it was too late. The fireball struck it square in the head. A sharp explosion erupted, and flames spread across its scales like a molten wave. The beast screamed in agony. Its body convulsed violently, tail lashing wildly through the air as a rasping shriek tore from its throat. The heated air trembled while its scales blackened and cracked beneath the inferno. The lizard collapsed. Cooked. The magical flames slowly faded, turning into thin ribbons of smoke. The air remained heavy with heat and the stench of burning flesh. Rowena and Aaliyah stood several steps away from the dead creature, breathing hard. Neither of them took their eyes off the lizard. Its massive body lay motionless in the sand, yet both women remained tense and ready, waiting to see if it would move again. Several long seconds passed. Nothing. Only the wind stirred the sand gently around the charred scales. Once it became clear that the beast would not rise again, the tension slowly drained from their bodies. The flames surrounding Aaliyah finally died out completely, and her shoulders relaxed slightly. Rowena immediately stepped closer and gave her a quick, practiced look from head to toe. She gently touched her arm, as though checking for pain or hidden injuries. “Are you alright?” she asked quietly. Aaliyah nodded, her breathing still uneven, though her gaze remained steady. “Yeah. Not even a scratch. I’m starting to think you’re cursed, Rowena,” Aaliyah said with a laugh. “Ever since I met you, I haven’t had a single peaceful day.” “Maybe I am,” Rowena replied. “But you’re insane for staying.” Aaliyah smiled. “Or maybe I’ve just gotten used to the chaos.” When they sat down once more beside the dying fire, both of them fell silent. Far away beyond the dunes, a moving shadow appeared. And with it came a strange, heavy rhythm. Footsteps. “That’s not an animal,” Aaliyah said quietly. Rowena nodded. “No. It’s something else.” Without another word, they rose to their feet and silently watched the shadow slowly drawing closer.
