Chapter XVI. – Silver in the depths

Night descended upon the Sanctuary Oasis like a heavy silk veil. Moonlight cast a pale, mysterious glow across the lake’s surface, reflecting in uneven ripples. The surrounding tents swayed gently in the night breeze, their canvas whispering softly with the wind as though they were breathing. Distant sounds drifted through the air — the chiming of tiny metal bells, faint laughter, murmuring merchants — while the scent of spices and mint tea rose toward the sky. And yet, beneath it all, a sense of dread lingered in the air, invisible but suffocating. Something dark and hostile was drawing closer, and though it could not yet be seen, everyone could feel it in their bones. James sat upon a rocky ledge not far from the tent, legs drawn beneath him, elbows resting on his knees. His gaze remained fixed on the distance, where darkness seeped into the sand and buried its fingers within the golden dunes. The visions from the Spring of Knowledge still repeated endlessly in his mind. A chill spread through his body. It was not merely fear, but the crushing awareness of a fate greater than his own life — an ancient burden as old as the desert itself. Something rustled behind him. He did not need to turn to know who it was. Aaliyah. She sat quietly beside him without saying a word. In her hands, she carried two small objects wrapped carefully in cloth. She unfolded one of them and placed it between them. It was a crimson stone, smooth and polished like a drop of blood, set within a silver frame — a talisman from her homeland. “For protection,” she whispered, her voice barely audible. “A red stone from the Raging Wilderness. They say it can recognize the truth hidden behind lies.” At first, James only watched it pulse beneath the moonlight, as though it possessed a life of its own. “Thank you,” he finally said. “I think this place… that spring… showed me more than I wanted to see. We’ll need more than protection.” Aaliyah nodded, her gaze drifting toward the lake, whose waters seemed almost alive. “Lysara warned us,” she whispered. “Morr’kalil — that ancient darkness — is awakening. The Seal we already felt weakening in the desert is losing its strength.” “And we have to stop that darkness,” James finished quietly. For a moment, Aaliyah simply looked at him. The silence between them felt heavy. She drew a slow breath and glanced around at the sleeping oasis. Rowena stood at the edge of the lake, her figure blending into the dark shadows of the fig trees whose branches bent toward the water like ancient guardians. Her eyes reflected in the surface, yet she saw more than her own reflection. She could feel the water shifting gently beneath her fingertips, as though it were responding to her presence. It curled softly around her hand, caressing her skin and drawing her closer with a tender, almost maternal warmth. “Rowena?” a voice called behind her. Harim stood a short distance away, his eyes calm yet filled with gravity. She did not turn around. “The water… it speaks to me,” she said slowly, with unexpected certainty. “It told me where I must go.” Harim watched the lake’s surface for a long moment. “The Spring of Knowledge is older than we can imagine. It remembers things that time itself has long forgotten. If it truly speaks to you, then listen… and let it guide you.” Rowena tightened her grip around the orb. Its light was dim now, almost like the breathing of a sleeping creature. “We leave tomorrow,” she said calmly. Harim nodded. “Good. The darkness sleeping beneath our feet is awakening. It is searching for its children.” That night, none of them truly slept. Aaliyah held the talisman tightly, searching for certainty within its crimson stone. James lay on his back with a dagger in hand, his thoughts wandering between the past and the unknown future. Rowena sat within the half-darkness of the tent, eyes open, the orb resting on her lap and pulsing faintly. Outside, the wind grew stronger, and the lake — silver and restless — released soft, ominous splashes into the night. Kayan, a young guard stationed at the northern gate of the oasis, felt unease spreading through his body. Despite the cold night air, sweat slowly ran down his back. He had the terrible feeling that something was approaching him. Instinctively, he called into the darkness, “Who’s there?” No answer came. Only silence. And then — a whisper. A foreign language he did not understand with his mind, yet somehow understood every word of, as though he had known it since birth. “Open.” His heart began to pound violently. He stepped backward. “Let me in.” Coldness wrapped around his throat like an invisible hand. He could no longer breathe. And then the darkness came. The Shadow consumed him. Kayan’s body tensed, his eyes widening — and their color changed. Liquid gold flooded his irises until his gaze no longer looked human. A breath escaped his lips, deep and чужой, as though it no longer belonged to him. The shadows around him thickened and twisted into a deformed black armor covered in spikes, along with a spear and sword. The weapons were heavy and unbalanced. They had not been forged for human hands. Every movement suggested they could split a person in half with a single strike. And yet Kayan wielded them with terrifying ease. Kayan was no longer Kayan. He had become Zarveth. His expression was empty, his golden eyes devoid of emotion. Coldness radiated from beneath the armor, along with a power that did not belong to this world. Zarveth straightened slowly and let his gaze sweep across the darkened landscape of the oasis. And silently, he waited for his time to come.
