Chapter XVII. – Where the water leads

The sound of the curtain shifting announced Rowena’s return. Her face was paler than usual, her movements quiet, as though she had returned from a place where no one spoke aloud. Aaliyah sat wrapped in a light blanket, while James stood nearby with his arms crossed. “The water knows me,” Rowena said softly, yet with certainty. “It speaks to me.” Aaliyah shivered slightly. “What did it say?” “That evil is awakening, and the answers are not here. It showed me part of a map.” James frowned. “Part of a map? Did you recognize the place? Did it tell you anything else?” Rowena shook her head. “No. It said nothing more. We have to go to Stonegard Castle. That’s where we’ll find the answers.” At that moment, Harim entered the tent. His face was grave, and deep, pulsing fear lingered in his eyes. Slowly, he looked over Aaliyah, James, and Rowena, as though judging whether they were ready to hear what he was about to say. Aaliyah pulled the blanket closer around herself. James remained perfectly still, his expression tense, eyes fixed on Harim. Rowena stared at him intently, the orb clenched tightly in her hand. Harim drew a slow breath before speaking quietly. “You must be prepared. What happened tonight changes everything — not only for us, but for the entire world as we know it.” He paused for a moment before continuing. “The patrol sent to relieve two young guards at the northern gate found one of them dead. Torn apart. Covered in blood. His eyes were frozen wide open, as though the terror he felt before dying had become trapped inside them forever.” Harim rubbed his temples, his gaze briefly losing focus within the darkness of memory. “And the other boy…” he continued quietly, “was standing over the body. But he was no longer the young man they knew. The patrol barely recognized him — and not because of the blood covering him. His appearance had changed so completely that he looked foreign… impossible.” “His skin was black as coal. His eyes were golden — like molten metal. There was nothing human left in them. Only emptiness and an icy calm.” “He wore armor black as a moonless night, covered in sharp spikes. In his hands, he held a sword and a spear, both seemingly forged from pure darkness.” “The sword was broad and heavy, its blade swallowing every trace of light, as though it had devoured hope itself. The spear was long and jagged, like a thorn torn from the deepest abyss — a cruel and merciless instrument of pain.” Harim lifted his eyes, his expression wavering between calmness and horror. “He didn’t speak. He only looked around him. Then he slowly turned and stepped into the shadows. He did not leave — he vanished. As if the darkness itself had accepted him and hidden him within its endless embrace.” Silence filled the tent. Harim’s words hung in the air like an invisible cloud of darkness pressing against every heart. Aaliyah pulled the blanket tighter around her shoulders and stared toward the shadows in the corner of the tent. Her hands trembled slightly despite her efforts to remain calm. “How is that possible?” she finally whispered, her voice fragile. “How can someone become so… changed?” James stiffened, his hands curling into fists as his expression darkened. “This isn’t just a threat from outside anymore. It’s something creeping inward, among us. Something that takes our people and turns them into… something that has no place beneath the light.” Aaliyah slowly rose to her feet, determination burning in her eyes. “If that’s no longer the boy we knew, then we have to be even more careful. And we need to act quickly. Whatever darkness consumed him could consume others as well.” Her gaze met Harim’s. “What do we do now?” Harim nodded, his expression remaining grave, though a trace of resolve entered his voice. “We prepare for the worst. Stonegard Castle is our only hope — perhaps there you’ll find answers… and strength. But the journey will be dangerous, and whatever has already escaped may follow your every step.” The air inside the tent seemed to grow even heavier. Each of them felt the weight of the inevitable — the realization that their world would never be the same again, and that the darkness they had once sensed only as a whisper was now becoming a roaring presence at their side. Aaliyah slowly nodded and exhaled softly. “Then we have to be strong. For ourselves… and for everyone who still believes the light can prevail.” James straightened and cast a firm glance toward the tent entrance, where the night swallowed everything beyond. “We leave at dawn.” Aaliyah sat down once more, her gaze fixed on the darkness outside. “Whatever comes,” she said quietly, “we won’t face it alone. Light always finds its way, even through shadows.”
