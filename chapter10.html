Chapter X. – Warning

Suddenly, a figure emerged from the dark corners of the ancient ruined temple — a middle-aged woman with light brown hair streaked with silver, carefully tied into a neat bun. Her pale green eyes shone with wisdom and calmness. “Welcome,” she said, her voice firm and steady. “Congratulations on completing the trial.” The woman slowly stepped closer, her gaze lingering on all three of them. “My name is Lysara,” she introduced herself quietly, though unmistakable authority resonated within her tone. “A healer from the Tower of Pure Light who, nearly five hundred years ago, helped seal Morr'kalil — the entity whose darkness nearly consumed our world.” Her words carried weight, echoing through the temple like voices from another age. “I created the Fragment of Time you just passed through. It was meant to serve as a warning.” Her expression darkened slightly. “A new era is approaching — one in which you must become stronger, wiser, and more united than ever before. Each of us has a role to play in this game of fate.” She paused briefly. “Your journey is only beginning.” The silence that followed was not empty. It was filled with expectation. With determination. Aaliyah realized that no matter how difficult the road ahead might become, she was no longer alone. With James and Rowena beside her, she finally possessed the strength to begin again — this time with purpose, and with a heart finally open to real love and courage. Lysara’s expression grew more serious, her green eyes shadowed by concern buried deep beneath her calm exterior. “I must warn you,” she began slowly. “Morr'kalil’s chains are weakening. His darkness longs to return, and if it succeeds…” Her voice lowered. “…it will bring ruin upon the entire world.” From a small chest, she removed a tiny orb glowing with soft muted light and carefully handed it to Rowena. “This is a Sealing Orb,” she explained. “It contains part of the magical force that keeps Morr'kalil bound.” Rowena gently accepted the orb into her hands. “You must protect it. Find the remaining three. And when the time comes, all four must be used together to seal Morr'kalil once more if his darkness attempts to escape.” Aaliyah felt the crushing weight of responsibility settling upon them. “Unfortunately,” Lysara continued softly, “the time I have left is running short. I cannot remain here any longer, nor can I give you more answers.” Her gaze moved across each of them. “You must rely on yourselves now. Your strength. Your courage. Those are your only weapons against the darkness approaching this world.” Her voice softened into a whisper, as though she herself were already saying farewell to the little time she had left. “Remember this — you are not alone. The light can shine again… if you stand together.” With one final look filled with wisdom and quiet blessing, Lysara vanished as silently as she had appeared. And she left behind only silence — and the crushing feeling of a new responsibility resting upon their shoulders. The temple began to tremble, as though time itself had realized its purpose had finally been fulfilled. The once motionless walls shuddered beneath silent waves of magical energy. The faint light lingering within the darkened halls began to die away, while the silver runes carved into the stone slowly faded into nothingness. The Fragment of Time — Lysara’s final creation — was collapsing. Instinctively, Aaliyah pressed herself against James, seeking comfort and certainty within his presence while the world around them crumbled apart. His arm immediately wrapped around her with reassuring strength, reminding her she was no longer alone. Without hesitation, he extended his other arm and pulled Rowena closer as well. He held both women tightly against him, as though he intended to shield them from everything approaching — from darkness, from loss, even from the end of time itself. For a brief moment, Rowena stiffened in surprise at the gesture. But then she relaxed as she felt the quiet warmth and strength shared between them. The three of them remained there together, locked in one embrace while the collapsing reality around them slowly unraveled. Facing it all with silent determination. And then — everything disappeared.
