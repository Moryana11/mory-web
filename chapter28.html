Chapter XXVIII. – Infiltration

Beneath the darkened sky, the Shadow encountered a patrol of knights guarding the outer route toward Stonegard Castle. Its steps appeared unsteady, its expression frightened. The moment the knights noticed the lone figure, they immediately grew alert and formed a protective circle around it. “Halt!” the captain commanded sharply. “Who are you, and what are you doing alone in the desert?” The Shadow lifted tearful eyes filled with perfectly crafted despair. Its voice trembled with fear — masterfully performed. “I’m a student of magic from the Tower of Pure Light… Please…” she gasped shakily. “Our caravan was attacked. My master… the other healers… they’re all dead. A man in black armor… he killed them.” She struggled for breath. “I escaped… I need help.” The knights exchanged uneasy glances. Some instinctively reached for their weapons, others for protective amulets hanging beneath their cloaks. “You spoke of a man in black armor carrying a spear and sword?” the captain asked, visibly disturbed. “Yes…” the Shadow whispered, trembling convincingly. “That demon… he slaughtered them…” Her voice cracked. “But if you hurry, perhaps you can still grant peace to their souls. Or… find traces. Please.” After a brief discussion, the patrol divided. Two of the knights, including the captain himself, mounted their horses with the Shadow. “We’ll take you to Stonegard Castle,” the captain decided. “The others will verify your story at the site of the attack.” “Thank you,” the Shadow whispered weakly. “Thank you… you saved me.” Far away, beyond the crest of the dunes, Zarveth sat upon a raised cliff and silently watched as his bait rode away alongside the knights. “A fine hunt,” he murmured with satisfaction. The sky above him darkened further, as though the night itself approved of his success. “The Shadow is inside,” he said quietly, almost reverently. “And soon… very soon… we will break the first seal.” Then he rose. And vanished into the darkness without a trace — as though he had been nothing more than another whisper of the desert.
