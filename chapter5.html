Chapter V. – Fire, shadow, and water

The desert wind changed. Rowena felt it first — not physically, but through her magic. The waves of time and pain that had breathed through the sands until now suddenly quieted. In their place came pressure. Invisible. Focused. A presence that belonged neither to the living nor the dead. Something in between. The figure approached slowly. A long spear in hand, daggers at his waist, and light black armor covering his body. Short dark hair tousled by the wind. A face carved from stone with rough stubble along his jaw, and black eyes that carried death within them. Beside her, Aaliyah stopped moving entirely. Her fingers tightened gently around her staff — not out of defense. Because of him. “Who is that?” Rowena whispered. Aaliyah nodded faintly. “James.” When he finally reached them, he stood in silence for a moment. He understood far too well what a single wrong word could cause. Aaliyah moved first. Slowly, tension held tightly in her shoulders, though there was no fear in her eyes. Only old uncertainty. “You disappeared for a long time,” she said. “I followed the tracks,” he answered quietly. “Mine?” “Yours. And the tracks of something that should never have come here.” Rowena could feel the tension crackling between them — powerful, ancient, unnamed. Part of her wanted to step away, but she remained where she was. Not as an intruder. But as a witness. As someone who needed to hear all of this. James quietly sat beside the fire across from Aaliyah. His black armor rustled softly as he rested his hands upon his knees and stared wordlessly into the flames. Half his face remained hidden in shadow, but his eyes — dark and deep — followed the movement of the fire with strange calmness. Aaliyah lowered her gaze and absentmindedly began fiddling with the strap of her boot. “What really brought you here, James?” Rowena finally asked, unable to contain her curiosity any longer. “Darkness,” he replied. “Someone… or something… crawled out from the depths beneath the Moonlight Desert. I can feel it. And the fact that you two are here isn’t a coincidence.” “So now you hunt shadows instead of people?” Aaliyah smirked. “Shadows don’t have souls. They don’t scream when they die,” he replied without a trace of humor. Aaliyah lowered her eyes. She had no answer to that. That very night, the desert split open. Not with sound. But with presence. From beneath the sands emerged the first night creature — a being formed from sand, bones, and magic older than the desert itself. Its eyes burned with blue light. The sound it emitted tore through the air like something meant to shatter sanity itself. All three of them rose at once. Aaliyah raised her staff. Behind her, Rowena quietly whispered a protective spell. And James, without saying a single word, lifted his spear and charged forward — a shadow moving between flame and water.
