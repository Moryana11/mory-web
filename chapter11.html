Chapter XI. – Return

Eyes opened into the harsh sunlight. Aaliyah narrowed her eyes as she realized the burning sand beneath her feet. The air was dry, scorching, and heavy — so different from the cold, silent temple where the last trace of Lysar’s magic had just faded away. They were back. In the middle of the desert. James was still holding both women in his arms. For a moment, they remained exactly as the Fragment of Time had left them, until they were certain the desert surrounding them was not another illusion. Rowena was the first to carefully pull away. She looked around — the dunes appeared the same as when they had entered the temple — yet something felt different. The air itself seemed heavier, not calm, but tense. “We’re back,” she stated flatly. Aaliyah knelt down and clenched a handful of sand, letting it slip through her fingers. It was warm. Burning. Real. “It almost feels like nothing has changed,” she said quietly. But when she looked at the glowing orb in Rowena’s hand, she knew everything was different. James scanned the horizon, the shadows of the dunes, and the rays of sunlight reflecting from the heated ground. “I don’t know how much time has passed,” he muttered. “But if Morr’kalil is weakening his bond, we need to act quickly.” Rowena nodded. “We need to find the nearest shelter. And a map. Then we figure out where the seals are located.” Aaliyah rose to her feet, her hands clenched into fists. Her heart beat stronger than before — not from fear, but from the realization that she was no longer a girl who merely followed others. She was part of something greater. James motioned for them to follow. “We’ll head east. I remember the Sanctuary Oasis is about a day’s journey from here. Assuming we survive the thirst.” They set out across the dunes, the hot air rippling above the sandy ridges while their shadows stretched into the distance. They walked together — three travelers in the wasteland, carrying a light in the form of a small orb that could decide the fate of the entire world.
